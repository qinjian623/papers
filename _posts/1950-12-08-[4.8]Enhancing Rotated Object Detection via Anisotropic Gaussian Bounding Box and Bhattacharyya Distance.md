# [4.8] Enhancing Rotated Object Detection via Anisotropic Gaussian Bounding Box and Bhattacharyya Distance

- Authors: Chien Thai, Mai Xuan Trang, Huong Ninh, Hoang Hiep Ly, Anh Son Le
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.16445v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.16445v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 Perception / 2D Rotated Object Detection
## Reason for Interest

The paper introduces a mathematically sound regression loss (Bhattacharyya Distance) and a novel Anisotropic Gaussian representation to address orientation ambiguity in square-like objects. The theoretical derivation and analysis of metric properties (triangle inequality, symmetry) are solid. However, the strict scoring penalty is applied because the experiments are exclusively conducted on Aerial/Remote Sensing datasets (DOTA, HRSC2016) with top-down orthographic imagery. There is no validation on ego-centric or car-end autonomous driving datasets (e.g., nuScenes, Waymo, KITTI), nor validation in a BEV perception setup. While the method has potential transferability to BEV detection, the paper is fundamentally a remote sensing computer vision paper, not a direct car-end autonomous driving study. Additionally, the baselines used (RetinaNet, R3Det) are relatively dated architectures.
