# [8.2] Comprehensive Assessment of LiDAR Evaluation Metrics: A Comparative Study Using Simulated and Real Data

- Authors: Syed Mostaquim Ali, Taufiq Rahman, Ghazal Farhani, Mohamed H. Zaki, Benoit Anctil, Domini...
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.02994v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.02994v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 Simulation / Sim2Real Fidelity Evaluation
## Reason for Interest

The paper tackles the critical industry problem of validating Virtual Testing Environments (VTE) for autonomous driving. It provides a rigorous, comprehensive assessment of geometric similarity metrics (Chamfer, DCD, ICP, etc.) to quantify the Sim2Real gap for LiDAR. 

Strengths:
1. **Systematic Evaluation**: Thorough sensitivity analysis of metrics against noise, outliers, density, and distortion, identifying Density Aware Chamfer Distance (DCD) as the most robust metric.
2. **Practical Validation**: Goes beyond synthetic tests to build a Digital Twin from real-world data (Toyota RAV4/Velodyne), demonstrating the workflow for sensor qualification.
3. **Insightful Findings**: Highlights the significant impact of LiDAR intensity on perception model performance (mIoU dropped to 4.7% without correction, 21% with correction), a factor often overlooked in geometric-only comparisons.

Weaknesses:
- The Digital Twin generation relies on standard meshing (Poisson) which resulted in significant gaps for complex objects like vegetation, though this served to test the metrics' sensitivity.
- It applies existing metrics rather than inventing a novel mathematical formulation, but the application to AD V&V is highly relevant.
