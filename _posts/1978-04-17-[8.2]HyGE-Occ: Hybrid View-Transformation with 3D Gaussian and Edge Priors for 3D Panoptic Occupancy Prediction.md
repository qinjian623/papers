# [8.2] HyGE-Occ: Hybrid View-Transformation with 3D Gaussian and Edge Priors for 3D Panoptic Occupancy Prediction

- Authors: Jong Wook Kim, Wonseok Roh, Ha Dam Baek, Pilhyeon Lee, Jonghyun Choi, Sangpil Kim
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.19871v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.19871v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 3D Panoptic Occupancy Prediction (Camera-based)
## Reason for Interest

The paper addresses a critical limitation in current BEV-based occupancy networks: depth quantization artifacts and blurred object boundaries. 

1. Innovation: The proposed 'Hybrid View-Transformation' cleverly combines the spatial precision of discrete LSS with the geometric continuity of Gaussian-based depth modeling. The addition of an explicit Edge Prediction Module supervised by semantic boundaries is a practical and effective geometric prior.
2. Performance: Experiments on the standard Occ3D-nuScenes benchmark demonstrate consistent improvements across all metrics (RayIoU, mIoU, RayPQ) over strong baselines like Panoptic-FlashOcc and ALOcc.
3. Practicality: The method maintains real-time inference speeds (30+ FPS for the base model), making it highly relevant for deployment on autonomous vehicles. 
4. Credibility: The ablation studies clearly isolate the contributions of the hybrid branch and edge supervision, validating the design choices.
