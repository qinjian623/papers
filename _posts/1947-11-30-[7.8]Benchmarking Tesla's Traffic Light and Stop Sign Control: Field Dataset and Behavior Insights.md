# [7.8] Benchmarking Tesla's Traffic Light and Stop Sign Control: Field Dataset and Behavior Insights

- Authors: Zheng Li, Peng Zhang, Shixiao Liang, Hang Zhou, Chengyuan Ma, Handong Yao, Qianwen Li, Xi...
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11802v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.11802v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 ADAS Behavior Modeling / Interaction with Traffic Control Devices
## Reason for Interest

The paper addresses a significant gap in the field by providing the first dedicated dataset and empirical analysis of ADAS interactions with traffic lights and stop signs (TCDs). 

Strengths:
1. **Novelty in Data**: Fills a void where existing datasets (Waymo, nuScenes) focus on ADS or human drivers, lacking specific ADAS-TCD interaction data.
2. **Behavioral Insights**: Quantitatively characterizes Tesla's operational logic (e.g., identifying a ~90m detection threshold for switching between car-following and signal-response modes).
3. **Simulation Value**: Calibrated FVDM parameters provide ready-to-use models for mixed-traffic simulations.

Weaknesses:
1. **Scale**: The dataset is relatively small (74 trajectory segments), limiting statistical robustness compared to large-scale open datasets.
2. **Scope**: The analysis is specific to one version of Tesla's software, which may change with OTA updates, limiting long-term generalizability.
