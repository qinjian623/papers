# [9.1] QueryOcc: Query-based Self-Supervision for 3D Semantic Occupancy

- Authors: Adam Lilja, Ji Lan, Junsheng Fu, Lars Hammarstrand
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.17221v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.17221v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 3D Semantic Occupancy Prediction / Self-supervised Learning
## Reason for Interest

The paper presents QueryOcc, a novel framework that shifts self-supervised occupancy learning from implicit rendering consistency (NeRF-style) to direct 4D spatio-temporal query supervision. Key strengths include: 1) Significant performance improvement (+26% RayIoU) over state-of-the-art methods like GaussianFlowOcc. 2) Introduction of a contractive BEV representation that efficiently handles unbounded scenes and long-range perception without exploding memory usage. 3) Practicality for autonomous driving with real-time inference (11.6 FPS) and flexibility to use both camera-only (via VFM pseudo-labels) and LiDAR supervision. 4) Extensive ablation studies verifying scalability with resolution and dataset size. The work addresses critical bottlenecks in labeling costs and scene representation.
