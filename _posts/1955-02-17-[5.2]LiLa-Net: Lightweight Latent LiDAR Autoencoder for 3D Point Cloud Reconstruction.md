# [5.2] LiLa-Net: Lightweight Latent LiDAR Autoencoder for 3D Point Cloud Reconstruction

- Authors: Mario Resino, Borja Pérez, Jaime Godoy, Abdulla Al-Kaff, Fernando García
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.02028v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.02028v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 3D Perception / Point Cloud Reconstruction & Compression
## Reason for Interest

The paper presents a practical lightweight autoencoder (LiLa-Net) for LiDAR data, which is relevant to on-vehicle autonomous driving constraints. However, the research value is significantly limited by two factors: 1) Experimental Rigor: The authors rely on a proprietary dataset collected at their lab for the primary automotive evaluation, ignoring established industry standards like nuScenes, Waymo, or KITTI. This makes the claimed improvements in reconstruction quality verifiable and isolated from domain bias. 2) Performance: The method acknowledges it does not match SOTA performance on standard benchmarks (ModelNet) compared to modern Transformer-based approaches (Point-BERT, Point-MAE). The innovation is relatively incremental (skip connections in a 1D-Conv autoencoder), and the baselines for reconstruction (FoldingNet, AE-EM) are dated.
