# [8.5] RadarGaussianDet3D: An Efficient and Effective Gaussian-based 3D Detector with 4D Automotive Radars

- Authors: Weiyi Xiong, Bing Zhu, Tao Huang, Zewei Zheng
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.16119v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.16119v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 4D Radar Perception / 3D Object Detection
## Reason for Interest

The paper presents a highly innovative application of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) to 4D radar-based object detection, addressing the modality's inherent sparsity. The proposed Point Gaussian Encoder (PGE) and Box Gaussian Loss (BGL) are theoretically sound and well-motivated. The experimental validation is robust, covering two major datasets (TJ4DRadSet, VoD) and providing detailed efficiency benchmarks on various hardware (including embedded systems). Although it does not strictly hold the top spot for absolute accuracy (narrowly trailing MAFF-Net), its ability to maintain high accuracy while more than doubling the inference speed makes it exceptionally valuable for practical autonomous driving systems.
