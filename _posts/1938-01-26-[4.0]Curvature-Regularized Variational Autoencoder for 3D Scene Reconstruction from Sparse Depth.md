# [4.0] Curvature-Regularized Variational Autoencoder for 3D Scene Reconstruction from Sparse Depth

- Authors: Maryam Yousefi, Soodeh Bakhshandeh
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.05783v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.05783v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 3D Scene Reconstruction / Occupancy Prediction
## Reason for Interest

The paper proposes a curvature-regularized VAE for 3D reconstruction from sparse depth. While the core task (occupancy prediction from sparse data) is theoretically relevant to autonomous driving, the study is severely limited by its experimental setting: it relies exclusively on the indoor NYU Depth V2 dataset with a small spatial volume (3m), which does not represent vehicle-side scenarios. Crucially, the authors explicitly acknowledge they did not compare their method against established SOTA approaches (like OccDepth or MonoScene) and did not test on outdoor AD datasets (e.g., nuScenes). Therefore, its practical value and proven performance for autonomous driving are currently low.
