# [3.0] Improvement of Collision Avoidance in Cut-In Maneuvers Using Time-to-Collision Metrics

- Authors: Jamal Raiyn
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.21280)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.21280.pdf)

## Subfields
 规划控制 / 安全避障 (Planning & Control / Collision Avoidance)
## Reason for Interest

论文存在严重的逻辑矛盾和质量问题。1. 方法论描述不一致：摘要和引言多次声称结合了“深度学习（Deep Learning）”，但正文方法章节（Section 3.2）和伪代码（Figure 7）仅展示了基于运动学公式的传统规则（Rules-Based）计算，完全未提及神经网络架构、训练数据或模型细节，涉嫌误导或核心内容缺失。2. 创新性低：提出的RBA（Rules-Based Approach）本质上是标准的TTC安全阈值检查，与现有的ACC/AEB逻辑无本质区别。3. 写作质量差：文中存在多处拼写和语法错误（如 'velocitys', 'Relatiev', 'nob critical'），且图表分析停留在简单的TTC统计上，缺乏深度。4. 实验基于简单的仿真对比，无法支撑其声称的“智能”或“深度学习”贡献。
## Abstract: 
This paper proposes a new strategy for collision avoidance system leveraging Time-to-Collision (TTC) metrics for handling cut-in scenarios, which are particularly challenging for autonomous vehicles (AVs). By integrating a deep learning with TTC calculations, the system predicts potential collisions and determines appropriate evasive actions compared to traditional TTC -based approaches.
