# [8.0] Fast and Flexible Robustness Certificates for Semantic Segmentation

- Authors: Thomas Massena, Corentin Friedrich, Franck Mamalet, Mathieu Serrurier
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.06010v1)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.06010v1.pdf)

## Subfields
 Perception / Semantic Segmentation / Safety & Robustness
## Reason for Interest

The paper presents a significant breakthrough in safety-critical perception by enabling real-time certified robustness for semantic segmentation, a task previously too computationally expensive for vehicle deployment using randomized smoothing. The use of Lipschitz-constrained networks allows for deterministic, fast certification (<0.1s). However, the score is capped because the method incurs a substantial drop in clean mIoU (51.80% vs 64.55% for the unconstrained baseline on Cityscapes) and relies on the older DeepLabV3 architecture, limiting immediate drop-in replacement potential for modern high-performance AD stacks.
