# [9.2] Dynamic Model Selection for Trajectory Prediction via Pairwise Ranking and Meta-Features

- Authors: Lu Bowen
- [arXiv Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.00126)
- [PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.00126.pdf)

## Subfields
 轨迹预测 / 动态模型选择与融合 / 鲁棒性增强
## Reason for Interest

这篇论文解决了自动驾驶中轨迹预测模型在复杂长尾场景下可靠性不足的核心问题，提出了一个创新性的动态多专家门控框架。其主要创新点包括：
1.  **创新的 meta-feature 使用：** 首次提出利用模型内部信号（如不确定性、稳定性、物理违规率）作为元特征，而非仅仅依赖几何场景描述符，显著提升了专家选择的有效性。
2.  **新颖的 pairwise-ranking 任务：** 将专家选择问题建模为成对排序任务，有效规避了传统回归或分类方法在处理高方差误差分布和类别不平衡时的校准和鲁棒性问题。
3.  **LLM 增强的混合决策：** 引入轻量级 LLM 作为语义监督器，在门控网络置信度低或遇到高风险语义场景时进行干预，结合了统计可靠性和可解释的语言级推理，显著提高了系统在安全关键场景下的性能和透明度。

实验部分非常完整和可信。论文在 nuPlan-mini 数据集上进行了全面的闭环和开环评估，包括对专家池、元特征、门控机制、LLM 监督器等关键组件的详尽消融实验。结果一致且有力地支持了所提方法的有效性，尤其是在提升长尾场景可靠性方面的表现突出。此外，论文清晰地讨论了部署考虑、计算开销和 LLM 延迟等实际挑战，并坦诚地列举了限制，这体现了严谨的研究态度和对行业应用的深刻理解。

综合来看，该工作对自动驾驶的轨迹预测领域具有重要的理论和实际价值，为构建更鲁棒、更安全的自动驾驶系统提供了可行路径。其严谨的实验设计、深入的分析和对实际部署挑战的考量，使其成为一篇高质量的论文。
## Abstract: 
Recent deep trajectory predictors (e.g., Jiang et al., 2023; Zhou et al., 2022) have achieved strong average accuracy but remain unreliable in complex long-tail driving scenarios. These limitations reveal the weakness of the prevailing "one-model-fits-all" paradigm, particularly in safety-critical urban contexts where simpler physics-based models can occasionally outperform advanced networks (Kalman, 1960). To bridge this gap, we propose a dynamic multi-expert gating framework that adaptively selects the most reliable trajectory predictor among a physics-informed LSTM, a Transformer, and a fine-tuned GameFormer on a per-sample basis.
  Our method leverages internal model signals (meta-features) such as stability and uncertainty (Gal and Ghahramani, 2016), which we demonstrate to be substantially more informative than geometric scene descriptors. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work to formulate trajectory expert selection as a pairwise-ranking problem over internal model signals (Burges et al., 2005), directly optimizing decision quality without requiring post-hoc calibration.
  Evaluated on the nuPlan-mini dataset (Caesar et al., 2021) with 1,287 samples, our LLM-enhanced tri-expert gate achieves a Final Displacement Error (FDE) of 2.567 m, representing a 9.5 percent reduction over GameFormer (2.835 m), and realizes 57.8 percent of the oracle performance bound. In open-loop simulations, after trajectory horizon alignment, the same configuration reduces FDE on left-turn scenarios by approximately 10 percent, demonstrating consistent improvements across both offline validation and open-loop evaluation. These results indicate that adaptive hybrid systems enhance trajectory reliability in safety-critical autonomous driving, providing a practical pathway beyond static single-model paradigms.
